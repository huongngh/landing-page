The giant panda
Panda is a bear species endemic to China. It is characterised by its bold black-and-white coat and rotund body. The name "giant panda" is sometimes used to distinguish it from the red panda, a neighboring musteloid.
Some random information
Taxonomic: Carnivoran. The giant panda has the digestive system of a carnivore, as well as carnivore-specific genes.
Diet: Primarily bamboo (around 99%), that are rich in starch and have up to 32% protein content.
Behavior: Primarily spends its life roaming and feeding in the bamboo forests.
Communication: Scent marks are used to spread these chemical cues and are placed on landmarks like rocks or trees.
